Must Visit Destinations

Srinagar: The summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir, known for its serene Dal Lake, Mughal gardens like Shalimar Bagh and Nishat Bagh, and the historic Jama Masjid.

Gulmarg: Famous for its picturesque landscapes, Gulmarg is a popular skiing destination in winter and offers beautiful meadows and trekking opportunities in summer

Pahalgam: This picturesque town is known for its lush greenery, serene rivers, and as the base camp for the Amarnath Yatra pilgrimage.

Jammu City: The winter capital of Jammu and Kashmir, known for its historic temples like Vaishno Devi and Raghunath Temple, as well as the majestic Bahu Fort.

Famous Cuisines

Rogan Josh: A signature dish of Kashmiri cuisine, Rogan Josh is a flavorful lamb dish cooked in a rich gravy of yogurt, spices, and infused with the flavor of Kashmiri red chilies

Yakhni: Yakhni is a traditional Kashmiri dish made with tender pieces of meat (usually chicken or mutton) cooked in a yogurt-based gravy seasoned with spices like fennel, cloves, and cardamom.

Gushtaba: Considered a delicacy, Gushtaba is a dish made of minced mutton balls cooked in a flavorful yogurt-based gravy. It is often served during special occasions and festivals.

Festivals

Lohri: Celebrated in January, Lohri marks the end of winter and the beginning of longer days. It is celebrated with bonfires, traditional music, and dancing.

Baisakhi: Celebrated in April, Baisakhi is a significant festival for the Sikh community. It marks the harvest season and is celebrated with great fervor, including processions, folk dances, and feasting.

Maha Shivaratri: Celebrated in February-March, Maha Shivaratri is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Devotees visit temples, perform rituals, and offer prayers to seek blessings.
