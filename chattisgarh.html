Must Visit Destinations

Chitrakoot Falls: Also known as the Niagara of India, Chitrakoot Falls is one of the largest waterfalls in India, offering breathtaking views and a serene ambiance

Bastar: Known for its tribal culture and handicrafts, Bastar is home to ancient temples, lush forests, and vibrant weekly markets

Tirathgarh Falls: Located amidst dense forests, Tirathgarh Falls is a picturesque waterfall with a series of cascades, ideal for nature lovers and photographers.

Raipur: The capital city, known for its bustling markets, historic sites like Mahant Ghasidas Memorial Museum, and vibrant culture.

Jagdalpur: Famous for its traditional dances, tribal festivals, and attractions like Chitrakote Waterfalls and Bastar Palace

Famous Cuisines

Chhattisgarhi Thali: A traditional meal featuring rice, dal, sabzi (vegetables), bhajis, and regional specialties like fara, chila, and rice pakoras

Petha: A popular sweet made from ash gourd, often flavored with saffron, rose, or cardamom, and enjoyed as a dessert.

Red Ant Chutney: A unique and spicy chutney made from red ants, traditionally consumed with rice or roti.

Chausela: A nutritious and flavorful dish made from steamed rice flour, often served with spicy chutney or curry

Bafauri: A protein-rich snack made from ground chana dal (split chickpeas), mixed with spices, and steamed to perfection

Festivals

Bastar Dussehra: Celebrated in Jagdalpur, this unique festival lasts for over 75 days and showcases the rich cultural heritage of the Bastar region through traditional dances, music, and rituals.

Hareli: Celebrated to mark the beginning of the sowing season, Hareli is a vibrant festival featuring feasts, folk dances, and rituals to invoke blessings for a bountiful harvest.

Madai Festival: A tribal festival celebrated in various parts of Chhattisgarh, characterized by colorful processions, tribal dances, and traditional music

Chakradhar Samaroh: Held in Raigarh, this cultural festival celebrates classical music and dance, attracting renowned artists and enthusiasts from across the country.

Chhattisgarh Rajyotsava: Celebrated on 1st November to commemorate the formation of Chhattisgarh as a separate state, this festival showcases the state's cultural diversity through various cultural programs and events.
