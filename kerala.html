Must Visit Destinations

Alleppey (Alappuzha): Famous for its backwaters, Alleppey offers houseboat cruises through a network of canals and lagoons. The serene environment and lush greenery make it a perfect place to unwind and experience Kerala's unique ecosystem.

Munnar: This hill station is renowned for its tea plantations, misty hills, and lush valleys. Visitors can enjoy trekking, nature walks, and breathtaking views of the Western Ghats.

Kochi (Cochin): A vibrant port city with a rich history influenced by various cultures including Dutch, Portuguese, and British. Explore the historic Fort Kochi area, visit the Jewish Synagogue, and witness the famous Chinese fishing nets.

Famous Cuisines

Sadya: Sadya is a traditional Kerala feast served on a banana leaf, usually during special occasions and festivals like Onam. It consists of a variety of vegetarian dishes, such as sambar, avial (a mixed vegetable curry), olan, thoran (stir-fried vegetables with coconut), pachadi (yogurt-based side dish), and pickles, served with rice and desserts like payasam.

Appam with Stew: Appam is a fermented rice pancake with a soft center and crispy edges, often served with a creamy and aromatic stew made with coconut milk, vegetables, and sometimes chicken or beef.

Fish Moilee: A creamy and mildly spiced coconut milk-based curry with fish, usually served with rice or appam.

Malabar Parotta with Chicken/Beef Curry: Malabar parotta is a flaky, layered flatbread made from refined flour, usually served with a spicy and flavorful chicken or beef curry.

Festivals

Onam: Onam is the most significant festival in Kerala, celebrated annually to welcome the mythical King Mahabali. It is a harvest festival and lasts for ten days, featuring various cultural performances, traditional games, and elaborate feasts known as Onam Sadya. The highlight of Onam is the grand procession called Thiruvonam, which includes colorful decorations, traditional music, and dance performances.

Thrissur Pooram: Thrissur Pooram is one of the most famous temple festivals in Kerala, held annually at the Vadakkunnathan Temple in Thrissur. It is known for its grandeur and spectacle, featuring a procession of decorated elephants, traditional percussion music, and fireworks displays. The festival attracts thousands of spectators from all over the world.

Nehru Trophy Boat Race: The Nehru Trophy Boat Race is an annual snake boat race held on the Punnamada Lake in Alappuzha. It is one of the most popular boat races in Kerala and attracts thousands of spectators. Teams compete in traditional snake boats (Chundan Vallams) to win the prestigious Nehru Trophy.
