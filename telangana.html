Must Visit Destinations

Ramoji film city: Ramoji Film City is an integrated film studio facility located in Abdullahpurmet, Hyderabad, India. it is the largest film studio complex in the world and as such has been certified by the Guinness World Records.

Govalconda fort: Golconda was the principal capital of the Qutub Shahi kings. The inner fort contains ruins of palaces, mosques and a hill top pavilion, which rises about 130 meters high and gives a bird's eye view of other buildings. Golconda fort is undoubtedly one of most magnificent fortress complexes in India.

Sri Chilkur Balaji Temple: the temple was built half a millennium ago. Set in sylvan surroundings, the temple attracts thousands of pilgrims every year and is an ideal place for sequestered retreat and meditation. It enjoyed in the past, great days of pomp and glory.

Famous Cuisines

Hydrabadi Biryani: Hyderabadi chicken biryani is one of the most popular biryanis in India. It’s the cooking method that makes it so special and amazingly delicious.The authentic style of cooking Hyderabadi Biryani is slowly cooking it over Dum. It gets its taste as it cooks in its own juices in the aromatic steam along with authentic Hyderabadi spices.

Golichina Mamsam: Golichina roughly translated means “fry” in Telugu. As Telangana cuisine is well-known for its spices, this dish incorporates succulent pieces of mutton into a thick gravy that is loaded with locally grown spices and cooked over low flame for hours so the meat can truly absorb the essence of the curry.

Malidalu: Malidalu is made of chapathi pieces combined with a mix of jaggery, cashews, pista and ghee and shaped into a small balls. Fully packed with nutrients, it is the best solution for anyone craving for a sweet after a meal.

Festivals

Bonalu: Bonalu, a vibrant Telangana festival celebrated during July-August, is a grand tribute to Goddess Mahakali. The air is filled with devotion and joy as communities gather to offer reverence.

Ugadi (March-April) | New Year, Fresh Beginnings: Ugadi marks the Telugu New Year, observed during March-April. The essence of fresh beginnings is embraced with traditional rituals and feasts. Cultural festivals in Telangana shine bright with Ugadi's celebratory spirit as families come together to indulge in festive delicacies and exchange wishes for prosperity.

Sammakka Sarakka Jatara: Sammakka Sarakka Jatara, celebrated in February, is a grand tribal spiritual festival. With deep-rooted reverence, tribal communities gather to pay homage to goddesses. The heart of Telangana festival celebrations
