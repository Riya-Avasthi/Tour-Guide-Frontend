Must Visit Destinations

Zakir Hussain Rose Garden :It's named after India's former President, Zakir Hussain. Thisbeautiful garden was set up in 1967. The park is spread over 10 acres and exhibits some of rarest and exquisite varieties of rose and is home to 1600 varieties of flowers.

Le Corbusier Centre :esigned by renowned architect Le Corbusier, this centre stands as a testament to his visionary ideas and modernist approach. The centre not only showcases the architect's work but also offers a glimpse into the city's history and cultural significance.

International Dolls Museum : Established in 1965 by K. Shankar Pillai, a famous cartoonist and illustrator, the museum houses a vast collection of dolls from various countries around the world. The museum boasts over 6,000 dolls from over 85 countries, making it one of the most diverse doll collections globally.

Famous Cuisines

Chole bhature :Chole Bhature is one of the most fulfilling and delicious meals which is significantly enjoyed by a majority of North Indian population, including Chandigarh. The delicious dish features the richness of thick, flavourful gravy prepared out of chickpeas and a myriad of spices

Amritsari Kulcha :mritsari Kulcha is an eternal North Indian delicacy, especially in Chandigarh. Kulcha is prepared with refined flour. In Chandigarh, you can relish a wide variety of Amritsari Kulcha, including potato kulcha, paneer kulcha, onion kulcha, creamy cheese kulcha, and so more.

Chiken tikka : It is one of the most famous street side delicacies in Chandigarh. The dish is prepared by mixing broiled chicken pieces that are deliciously marinated in yogurt and in a wide range of spices like coriander powder, turmeric, salt, cayenne pepper, cumin powder, ginger-garlic paste, and chillies. Seamless marination allows chicken pieces to absorb all the delightful spices, making it tender and juicy.

Festivals

Baisakhi: This festival marks the beginning of the harvest season. It usually falls in the month of April every year. People dress up in traditional dresses and celebrate this festival with music and folk dance.

Chrysanthemums Show :It is an annual flower festival of the December month in the Terrace garden of sector 33. The festival showcases a wide variety of flowers from different parts of the country. As a part of this festival, many gardening competitions are held.

Mango fest:Mango is an important Indian fruit and also our National Fruit. It is a one of kind festival where people who grow and trade mangoes get an opportunity to showcase their produce. Many agricultural universities also participate and exhibit the latest hybrids of mangoes.
