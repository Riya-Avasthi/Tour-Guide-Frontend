Must Visit Destinations

Mumbai: Mumbai, the capital city of Maharashtra, is known for its bustling streets, iconic landmarks like the Gateway of India and Marine Drive, vibrant culture, and delicious street food.

Pune: Pune, also known as the "Oxford of the East," is famous for its educational institutions, historical sites like Shaniwar Wada, and pleasant weather.

Aurangabad: Aurangabad is renowned for its UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the Ajanta and Ellora Caves, which showcase ancient Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain monuments.

Nashik: Nashik is popular for its temples, vineyards, and religious significance, particularly during the Kumbh Mela.

Famous Cuisines

Vada Pav: Vada Pav is a popular street food in Maharashtra consisting of a deep-fried potato patty served in a bun with chutneys.

Misal: Misal is a spicy curry made with sprouted moth beans (matki) and served with farsan (crunchy toppings) and pav (bread).

Pav Bhaji: Pav Bhaji is a fast-food dish made of mashed vegetables cooked in a spicy tomato-based gravy, served with buttered bread rolls.

Bombil Fry: Bombil Fry, also known as Bombay Duck Fry, is a crispy and flavorful fish fry made from fresh Bombay duck fish.

Festivals

Ganesh Chaturthi: Ganesh Chaturthi is a Hindu festival celebrating the birth of Lord Ganesha, marked with grand processions, idol immersion ceremonies, and cultural events.

Diwali: Diwali, the festival of lights, is celebrated with the lighting of lamps, fireworks, exchanging sweets, and prayers to Goddess Lakshmi for prosperity and well-being.

Holi: Holi, the festival of colors, is celebrated with vibrant colored powders, water balloons, and festive gatherings to mark the triumph of good over evil and the arrival of spring.

Gudi Padwa: Gudi Padwa, also known as Maharashtrian New Year, is celebrated with the raising of Gudi flags, traditional rituals, feasting, and cultural performances to usher in prosperity and happiness.
