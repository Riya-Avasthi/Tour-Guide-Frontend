Must Visit Destinations

Amritsar: Home to the iconic Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib), one of the most sacred sites for Sikhs. Other attractions include the historic Jallianwala Bagh and the vibrant bazaars of the old city

Chandigarh: India's first planned city designed by Swiss-French architect Le Corbusier. Visit the Rock Garden, Sukhna Lake, and the Capitol Complex.

Patiala: Famous for its royal heritage, including the opulent Qila Mubarak complex and the beautiful gardens of Baradari Palace.

Amritsar Wagah Border: Witness the elaborate Beating Retreat ceremony between India and Pakistan, a daily military practice that draws crowds from both sides

Famous Cuisines

Sarson da Saag and Makki di Roti: A traditional Punjabi dish made with mustard greens and served with cornmeal flatbread.

Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani): A rich and creamy chicken curry cooked in a tomato and butter-based sauce

Tandoori Chicken: Marinated chicken cooked in a clay oven (tandoor), resulting in smoky and flavorful meat

Lassi: A refreshing yogurt-based drink, available in sweet or salty variations.

Festivals

Baisakhi: Celebrated in April, Baisakhi marks the Sikh New Year and the harvest festival. It's celebrated with fervor in Punjab with traditional dances, music, and fairs.

Lohri: Celebrated in January, Lohri marks the end of winter and the arrival of longer days. Bonfires, traditional songs, and dance are part of the festivities.

Gurpurab: Celebrations of the birth anniversaries of Sikh gurus, especially Guru Nanak Dev Ji, are grand affairs in Punjab with processions, religious rituals, and community meals (langar).
