Must Visit Destinations

Tirupati: Home to the famous Venkateswara Temple, one of the wealthiest and most visited religious sites in the world.

Visakhapatnam (Vizag): Known for its beautiful beaches like Ramakrishna Beach and the picturesque Araku Valley.

Amaravati: The capital region of Andhra Pradesh, known for its Buddhist heritage and the Amaravati Stupa.

Vijayawada: A bustling city on the banks of the Krishna River, known for its historic landmarks like Kanaka Durga Temple and Prakasam Barrage.

Guntur: Famous for its spicy cuisine and the ancient Undavalli Caves with rock-cut temples and intricate sculptures.

Famous Cuisines

Hyderabadi Cuisine: While Hyderabad is now part of Telangana, its cuisine is widely popular in Andhra Pradesh, featuring flavorful dishes like Hyderabadi biryani, haleem, and kebabs.

Andhra Cuisine: Known for its spicy and tangy flavors, with specialties like Andhra-style chilli chicken, pulihora (tamarind rice), gongura pachadi (sorrel leaves chutney), and the famous Andhra thali

Rayalaseema Cuisine: Features rich and spicy dishes like rayalaseema mutton curry, ragi sangati (millet balls), and kheema pulao

Coastal Andhra Cuisine: Highlights seafood delicacies like chepala pulusu (fish curry), prawn fry, and avakaya (mango pickle)

Festivals

Sankranti: Celebrated with kite-flying competitions, feasting, and traditional rituals across Andhra Pradesh.

Ugadi: The Telugu New Year, celebrated with special rituals, festive meals, and cultural events.

Vinayaka Chavithi: Also known as Ganesh Chaturthi, celebrated with the installation of Ganesha idols, prayers, and community gatherings.

Krishna Pushkaralu: A festival celebrated once every 12 years at various river banks, particularly the Godavari and Krishna rivers, with religious rituals and holy dips
