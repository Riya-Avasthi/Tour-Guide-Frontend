Must Visit Destinations

Jaipur: Known as the Pink City, Jaipur is the capital of Rajasthan and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visit the iconic Amber Fort, City Palace, Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), and Jantar Mantar observatory.

Udaipur: Often called the Venice of the East, Udaipur is known for its beautiful lakes and palaces. Don't miss the City Palace, Lake Palace (now a luxury hotel), Jag Mandir, and the serene Lake Pichola.

Jaisalmer: This desert city is famous for its golden sandstone architecture and the vast Thar Desert. Explore the majestic Jaisalmer Fort, known as the Golden Fort, and enjoy a camel safari in the desert.

Famous Cuisines

Dal Baati Churma: This iconic dish consists of three parts - dal (lentils), baati (hard wheat rolls), and churma (sweet powdered mixture). Baati is traditionally baked in a charcoal or wood-fired oven and then served with dal and churma.

Gatte ki Sabzi: A popular vegetarian dish made with gram flour (besan) dumplings cooked in a spicy yogurt-based gravy. It's flavored with various spices like cumin, coriander, and turmeric.

Ker Sangri: A traditional Rajasthani dish made from dried desert beans (sangri) and berries (ker) native to the arid regions of Rajasthan. It's usually cooked with spices and served as a side dish with roti or rice.

Festivals

Pushkar Camel Fair: Held annually in the town of Pushkar, this fair is one of the largest cattle fairs in the world. It usually takes place in November and includes activities such as camel trading, cultural performances, camel races, and religious ceremonies.

Jaipur Literature Festival: Celebrated in the Pink City of Jaipur, this festival is one of the largest literary gatherings in the world. It usually occurs in January and features renowned authors, intellectuals, and thinkers from around the globe, along with cultural performances, music, and food

Jaisalmer Desert Festival: This three-day festival celebrates the culture and traditions of the Thar Desert. Held in February, it features folk music and dance performances, camel races, turban tying competitions, and a showcase of traditional Rajasthani attire amidst the beautiful backdrop of the Jaisalmer Fort.v
